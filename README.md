# Real Stone Age

A NeoForge mod for Minecraft that slows early survival down and pushes it back toward
an actual stone age. Wood and gold tools are gone, so your first tools come from rocks
and flint you find lying around. Copper gives you a rough, fragile mid-step, and once
you're ready for iron and diamond, you'll need to build a proper forge to get there —
a regular furnace just won't cut it anymore.

## Why

Vanilla's early game is over in minutes: punch a tree, make a crafting table, make
wooden tools, mine straight down to iron. Real Stone Age stretches that out and adds
some real texture to it — there's a genuine stone age before the bronze/iron age, tools
feel more disposable, and getting to "real" metalworking is a deliberate milestone
instead of a formality.

## What's different

- **No wood or gold tools.** Your first tools are stone, made from rocks and flint you
  scavenge off the ground — no crafting table or tree-punching required to get started.
- **Copper is a real, if fragile, stepping stone.** Raw copper can be hammered into
  tools and armor right away, but it comes out flimsy. Smelt it into ingots and forge
  it properly for the full-strength version.
- **Tools and armor feel different by tier.** Stone is weaker and more brittle than
  vanilla, iron is much tougher, and armor across the board leans further into
  protecting your legs and knocking enemies back.
- **A proper forge, not just a furnace.** Ores and stone can no longer be smelted in a
  regular furnace — you need a blast furnace. Iron and diamond gear now requires
  building a forge (an anvil paired with a blast furnace) rather than a crafting
  table.
- **New early-game items:** loose rocks and flint shards you can pick up off the
  ground, a bellows for building your first blast furnace, and a disposable,
  wood-free Crafting Bench so you're never stuck without a crafting grid.
- **Wood matters more.** Logs won't drop wood at all unless you're using an axe, and
  chopping wood without one is noticeably slower.
- **Ore near the surface is stingier.** Coal, iron, and copper found near the surface
  are scarcer and reveal less of the vein than they do underground, so shallow mining
  is a lot less rewarding than digging deep.
